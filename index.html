Crawl 4 The Cure

REDESIGN

Project Description: Crawl 4 The Cure is an event identified as it’s own non-profit organization which donates all it’s proceeds to the National Multiple Sclerosis Society - Upper Midwest Chapter.
Tools Used: Miro, InVision, Adobe XD
The Problem: The original design provided users with information that was uneasy to access and a website experience that was not responsive between devices.
The Solution: We reorganized the information in a hierarchical order, being sure to clearly address: what, when, where, how, why, and who.

Learn more

The Research Phase

Our group conducted 1-on-1 interviews as well as a Google survey of users on social media including Facebook and Instagram. Transcripts 1 & 2. Survey findings here.
Our research plan focused on these main topics:
1. I want to understand what information is most important to our users
2. I want to know what engages users when visiting a website
3. I want to understand what influences users toward supporting a causes
These topics directed our focus about keeping people informed about the event and increase registration. We also completed a competitor analysis. The results strongly pointed toward a desire for clear communication about the facts: Who, What, Where, When - Why & How being clear steps

Problem Statement:

The original design provided users with information that was uneasy to access and a website experience that was not responsive between devices.

The Iteration Phase

During usability testing 3 different users and received great feedback to make iterations to our final prototype. Those we implemented included:

  • Register button at the top of the homepage
  • Register in event details
  • An obvious home page button
  • Buttons under sponsor levels
  • Arrow to indicate the front page scrolls
  • Footer
  • Back button for the contact form
